Discover balinese food

Top 5 Balinese dishes to try

Nasi/Mie Goreng

Nasi Goreng and Mie goreng are fried rice and fried noodles. it is the most common local dish in Bali, and you will see it on the menus everywhere from your local street markets to restaurants. Precooked rice is usually friend in a small amount of oil then spiced with shallots, garlic, chili, or ginger and uses kecap manis which is a sweet soy sauce giving it the distinctive flavour of Indonesian fried rice. It can include meat and prawns and is commonly served with egg whether it's mixed in the rice or served on the side.

Sate

Satay or sate in Indonesian spelling is a southeast Asian dish of seasoned, skewered, and grilled meat served with a peanut sauce. It is a popular street food and It's origin was in the java island before it spread throughout Indonesia where it became one of there national dishes. It is also popular in many other southeast Asian countries including Brunei, Malaysia, Singapore, Philippines, and Thailand.

Sambal Matah

Originally from Bali sambal matah can be translated as raw sambal or raw chili sauce. Sambal is a classic Balinese accompaniment to any dish. its best enjoyed on fresh fish or chicken or even just dig in with a spoon.

Babi Guling

Babi Guling is the Indonesian version of a roast sucking pig. Although not common to other Indonesian regions, it is one of the most popular dishes in Bali where the predominant religion is Hinduism. Eating Babi Guling in Bali used to be reserved for special occasion sand ceremonies such as funerals and weddings. The Pig is stuffed and infused with a concoction of spices and then roasted over an open fire resulting in crispy skin and succulent meat.

Martabak

Martabak is a very popular the sweet in Indonesia and bali. It is like a Indonesian version of a pancake but much thicker and with fillings such as chocolate, condensed milk and cheese and peanut crunches. It has a fluffy and chewy texture with a rich buttery and sweet flavour.
